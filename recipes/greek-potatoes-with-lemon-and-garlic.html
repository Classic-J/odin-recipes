Greek Potatoes with Lemon and Garlic

Description

Behold, the Greek potatoe wedges garnished with savory garlic, lemon juice, and fresh parsley leaves. Once you sink your teeth into the potatoe you'll have to eat more than one. The crispy exterior pairs heavenly with the soft interior and the seasoning penetrates throughout the entire wedge to give everything a balanced, savory flavor.

That's not all though! This recipe is super easy and simple as well since you just need to stick the concoction in the oven. In as little as 15 minutes, you and your family can enjoy crispy golden goodness!

Ingredients

For the spice mix
  • 1 teaspoon Lawry's seasoned salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon sweet paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
For the potatoes
  • 4 large baking potatoes, (russet, Idaho, or Yukon gold), peeled, washed, cut into wedges
  • 8 large garlic cloves, chopped
  • 4 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil, plus more for coating
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 1 1/4 cups vegetable or chicken stock
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup parsley leaves, coarsely chopped

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F. Coat a large, 9”X13-inch baking dish in a thin layer of olive oil.
  2. Make the spice mix: In a small bowl, mix together the seasoned salt, black pepper, sweet paprika, and dried rosemary.
  3. Prep the potatoes: Place the potato wedges in the oiled baking dish and sprinkle with the spice mix. Toss to evenly to distribute the spices.
  4. Prep the broth: In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the chopped garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, and broth. Pour over the potatoes.
  5. Bake the potatoes: Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 40 minutes.
  6. Finish the potatoes: Remove the potatoes from the oven, uncover, and sprinkle on the Parmesan cheese. Return to the oven, uncovered, and roast until the potatoes are cooked through and have turned a nice golden brown with a little crust forming, about 10-15 minutes.
  7. Garnish and serve: Remove from the oven, garnish with a sprinkle of fresh parsley, and serve.
